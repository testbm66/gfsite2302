Why Solar in Edinburgh

Edinburgh gets more sun than you think

Many people assume Scotland doesn't get enough sunshine for solar panels. The truth is, modern solar panels work effectively even in overcast conditions, and Edinburgh's latitude means long summer days with up to 17 hours of daylight.

A typical Edinburgh household with a well-designed solar system can generate 3,500-4,500 kWh per year, covering a significant portion of their annual electricity needs.

  • ~1,400 hours of sunshine per year
  • Up to 17 hours of daylight in summer
  • Modern panels work in cloudy conditions
  • Typical 50-70% reduction in electricity bills
  • 6-8 year average payback period
